Duality for linear problems

Let's assume a problem is which we want to minimize the cost: $$ \textrm{min}\: c^T x $$ where \(c^T \in \mathbb{R}^n \) is the cost vector and \(x\in \mathbb{R}^n\) are the unknowns with constraints: $$ Ax\geq b \\ x \geq 0 $$ where \(A\) has dimension \(m\textrm(x)n\) and \(b\in \mathbb{R}^m\) The dual problem is defined as: $$ \textrm{max}\: b^Ty $$ with constraints: $$ A^Ty\leq c \\ y \geq 0 $$ where \(y \in \mathbb{R}^m\) are the unknowns of the dual problem.

Theorem 1

For admitted x and admitted y: $$c^Tx \geq b^Ty $$ because $$ c^T x \geq (A^T y)^Tx = y^TAx \geq y^Tb$$

Theorem 2

For optimal \(x^*\) and optimal \(y^*\): $$c^Tx^* = b^Ty^*$$ and if \((Ax^*)_i > b_i\) then \(y^*_i = 0\) and if \((A^Ty^*)_i < c_i\) then \(x^*_i = 0\)

Shadow costs

Let's suppose that we have a minimization linear problem with constraints as described preciously with an optimal solution \(x^*\) and its corresponding dual problem with optimal solution \(y^*\). If we modify the i-th constraint: $$ (Ax)_i \geq b_i + \epsilon_i $$ the optimal solution becomes: $$ x^* \rightarrow x^*+\epsilon_i\delta x^* $$
